About Flowers

Flowers are the reproductive structures found in flowering plants (angiosperms). They form seeds via the process of pollination and fertilization. Flowers vary widely in shape, color, scent, and size.

Parts of a Flower

  • Petals
  • Sepals
  • Stamens
  • Pistil (or Carpel)
  • Receptacle, Ovary, Style, Stigma

Interesting Facts

Some flowers open only at specific times of day. Some are pollinated by insects, others by wind. Flowers can also change color after pollination to deter repeat visits by pollinators.
